# Lo-Fi Hip Hop Drum Patterns

> Source: Perplexity Sonar | Batch Import | Category: styles

### 1. Rhythm & Groove (Syncopation, Swing)
Lo-Fi Hip Hop drum patterns use 16th-note grids in 4/4 time at 60-90 BPM, emphasizing **simple head-nodding grooves** with **swing** (10-20% on 16th notes) and **syncopation** on off-beats.[1][2][3][4]  
- **Boom Bap base**: Kick on 1, 3 (beats); Snare on 2, 4; Hi-hats on 8ths/16ths with swing (e.g., delay every 2nd 16th).[1][2][4][6]  
- **Halftime variant**: Kick on 1; Snare on 3; swung hi-hats for laid-back feel.[1][3]  
- **Syncopation**: Off-beat kicks/snares (e.g., 16th-note ghost notes before 2/4); "Dilla swing" or triplet friction on hi-hats.[1][2][7]  
Humanize with ±5-10ms timing offsets on 16ths.[1][3]

### 2. Harmony & Scales
**Jazzy minor/major pentatonic scales** (e.g., A minor pentatonic: intervals 0-3-5-7-10 semitones) or **Dorian mode** (0-2-3-5-7-9-10) for nostalgic loops; simple 4-chord progressions (ii-V-I-IV) at half-time feel.[3]  
Rooted in jazz/hip-hop; no complex modulations—repeat 2-4 bar phrases with detuned samples.[3]

### 3. Typical Instruments & Patterns (Kick, Snare, Bass)
- **Kick**: Soft, thumpy (vintage 808/LinnDrum emulations); on 1, 3 (or 1+syncopated 16ths); low-pass filtered.[1][2][3][5][6] Pattern: 1, 3, +1/16 before 3.  
- **Snare**: Snappy, loose/crunchy; on 2, 4 (+ghost 16ths); rimshots or claps layered.[1][3][4][5] Pattern: 2, 4, +ghost on 2.3/4.3 (16ths).  
- **Bass**: Subby 808-style (sine/root note); follows kick rhythm, octave jumps; sidechained to kick.[5] Pattern: sustains on 1-1.5, 3-3.5 (8th notes).  
- **Hi-hats/Rides**: Swung 8th/16th patterns (closed hats every 2nd 16th; open on upbeats); low-passed, varied.[1][3][4][6] Add shakers/tambourines on 16ths for groove.[1]

### 4. Velocity Dynamic Range
**Narrow range** (40-90 MIDI velocity) for muted, organic feel; avoid peaks over 100.[3][4]  
- Kick: 70-90 (thumpy, consistent).[3]  
- Snare: 60-85 (snappy but soft).[3][4]  
- Hi-hats: 40-70 (varied ±10 for humanize; low/consistent).[4]  
- Bass/Perc: 50-80; sidechain ducks 10-20dB on kick hits.[5] Overall compression + low-pass for dusty texture.[1][5]
