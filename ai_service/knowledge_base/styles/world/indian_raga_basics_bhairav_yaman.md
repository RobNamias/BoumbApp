# Indian Raga Basics (Bhairav, Yaman)

> Source: Perplexity Sonar | Batch Import | Category: styles/world

### 1. Rhythm & Groove (Syncopation, Swing)
Indian Raga Bhairav and Yaman use **Teen Taal** (16 beats, subdivided into 4 sections of 4 beats each) as a common cyclic rhythm for structured sections like jor or gat[2].  
- **16th-note patterns**: Slow tempos (e.g., 60-80 BPM) start with sparse 16th-note divisions in tabla bol patterns (e.g., Dha Dhin Dhin Dha for sum beat); build to faster 16th-note fills without fixed syncopation or swing—rhythms are straight and metric, emphasizing tihai (rhythmic cadences repeating 3 times to land on sam)[2].  
- No Western swing; groove relies on rubato in alap (unmetered intro) transitioning to steady taala[1][2].

### 2. Harmony & Scales
Ragas are **melodic modes** (no harmony/chords); focus on scalar ascents (**aaroah**) and descents (**avaroh**) with **vadi** (dominant note, emphasized) and **samvadi** (secondary note)[1][5].  
- **Raga Bhairav** (Bhairav Thaat): Scale intervals (C as Sa): C **Db(♭2)** E F G **Ab(♭6)** B (audav-shadav jati: 5 asc/6 desc notes). Aaroah: **S** ♭**R** G M P ♭**D** **S'**; Avroh: **S'** ♭**D** P M G ♭**R** S. Vadi: **Dha(♭6)**; Samvadi: **Re(♭2)**[1][3][7].  
- **Raga Yaman** (Kalyan Thaat): Scale intervals: C D E **F#(♯4)** G A B (sampoorna: 7 notes). Aaroah: **S** R G **M♯** P D **N** **S'**; Avroh: **S'** **N** D P **M♯** G R S. Vadi: **Ga(3)**; Samvadi: **Ni(7)**[1][2][3].  
Use gamakas (microtonal oscillations) on vadi notes in 16th-note phrases[1].

### 3. Typical Instruments & Patterns
No fixed kick/snare/bass (Western EDM terms); emulate with Indian acoustic roles in sequencer.  
- **Lead Melody**: Sitar/santoor/vocal—arpeggiate scale in 16th notes (e.g., Bhairav: ♭R-G-M-P-♭D; Yaman: R-G-M♯-P-D), hold vadi 2-4 16ths, add gamak wobble (pitch bend ±20 cents)[1][6].  
- **Rhythm (Tabla)**: "Kick" = Bayan (bass drum) on beats 1,5,9,13 (Dha); "Snare" = Tabla on offbeats 2,3,6,7,10,11,14,15 (Tin Na); 16th-note tukras (fills) like Dhit-Tek-Tad-Dhin[2].  
- **Drone (Tanpura)**: Sustained Sa-Pa (root-fifth) at low velocity, continuous 16th-note pulse emulation via subtle volume modulation[2].  
- **Support**: Sarangi (sustained slides) or flute (breath-y 16th-note runs on pakad phrases)[4].

### 4. Velocity Dynamic Range
Wide **pp to ff** (20-127 velocity) for emotional arc; slow builds from alap.  
- Alap/jor: Low velocity (20-60) on melody notes, sparse 16th notes[2].  
- Gat/jhala: Mid-high (70-100) on vadi/samvadi, peaks at 110-127 on tihai resolutions; rhythm layer 80-110 steady, accents (120+) on sum/bols[2].  
- Overall range: 40-unit spread per phrase, emphasizing crescendos on gamaks (velocity ramp +10 over 4 16ths)[1].
